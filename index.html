Road Accident Casualty Dashboard

Interactive Excel dashboard analysing road accident casualty data across 2021–2022. Built using pivot tables, slicers, donut charts, bar charts, and a line trend chart. Filters by year, accident date, and urban/rural area allow dynamic exploration of 417,883 total casualties.

Key analytical findings
  • Total casualties: 417,883 across the analysis period
  • Fatal casualties represent only 1.7% of total — slight injuries dominate at 83.9%
  • Single carriageway roads account for 309,698 casualties — the highest by road type
  • Dry road surfaces produce the most casualties (384,769) — counter-intuitive to expectation
  • Urban areas record significantly higher casualties than rural areas across both years

Publications & ongoing work
Graduate-level research output combining quantitative methods with supply chain theory

Supply Chain Risk Exposure and Organisational Performance: Evidence from a Manufacturing Firm in an Emerging Economy

Examines the differential effects of natural disaster, man-made, and infrastructural supply chain risks on organisational performance using Partial Least Squares Structural Equation Modelling (PLS-SEM). Sample: n = 300 manufacturing firm respondents. R² = 0.799 for the production delay model — indicating strong explanatory power.

Published — SSRN PLS-SEM n=300 April 2026
DOI: 10.2139/ssrn.6481582 →
